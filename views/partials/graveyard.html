Try to keep your back as staight and up as possible. The twisting motion of your hips might make you slouch and flop your arms around, and this looks goofy. Try to keep your body erect and your arms steady, close to your sides, bringing them up just a little for balance when needed.

With each kick out, relax your muscles and let your leg jerk outward. This snappiness accentuates the move, as your leg snaps out with each hit of the beat (in whatever song you're dancing to).

WALKTHROUGH
  • 1 From the toe divot stance (right leg back and slightly on ball of foot, left foot forward and on toe, both toes pointing right), bring your left foot very slightly to your right.
  • 2 Without your left leg going further than a foot away from the center of your stance, bring your left foot back towards the left, leading with your toe (imagine an eel slithering thru the water, turning left - your toe is it's head).
  • 3 Bring your foot all the way to the farthest left of your stance you can, in one snappy kicking motion.
  • 4 Without stopping, continue out of the snap and bring your foot back in towards your stance and across to your right.
  • 5 Now, kicking out to the farthest right of your stance you can, repeat the kicking motion.
  • 6 Repeat kicks however many times you want, snapping each time as your foot reaches the furthest out it can go to your sides.

As you rapidly twist your foot, your whole body will want to jostle around - try your best to keep the rest of your body still as you do the divot - only having the heel move and the rest of your body still/stiff is what makes the move look interesting. Your head will want to flop left and right, and your hips will want to twist as well - fight those urges.

Hand in hand will keeping your head and hips still, your arms will want to flop around as you do the motion. Try your best to keep your hands still, as otherwise they flop around and give the impression you're having a seizure (at least that's what I looked like). Try keeping them pinned to your side.

  • 1 Land in toe-heel stance of cuts (left foot forward right, right foot at back left)
