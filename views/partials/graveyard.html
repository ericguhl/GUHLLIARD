Try to keep your back as staight and up as possible. The twisting motion of your hips might make you slouch and flop your arms around, and this looks goofy. Try to keep your body erect and your arms steady, close to your sides, bringing them up just a little for balance when needed.

WALKTHROUGH
  • 1 From the toe divot stance (right leg back and slightly on ball of foot, left foot forward and on toe, both toes pointing right), bring your left foot very slightly to your right.
  • 2 Without your left leg going further than a foot away from the center of your stance, bring your left foot back towards the left, leading with your toe (imagine an eel slithering thru the water, turning left - your toe is it's head).
  • 3 Bring your foot all the way to the farthest left of your stance you can, in one snappy kicking motion.
  • 4 Without stopping, continue out of the snap and bring your foot back in towards your stance and across to your right.
  • 5 Now, kicking out to the farthest right of your stance you can, repeat the kicking motion.
  • 6 Repeat kicks however many times you want, snapping each time as your foot reaches the furthest out it can go to your sides.

As you rapidly twist your foot, your whole body will want to jostle around - try your best to keep the rest of your body still as you do the divot - only having the heel move and the rest of your body still/stiff is what makes the move look interesting. Your head will want to flop left and right, and your hips will want to twist as well - fight those urges.

Hand in hand will keeping your head and hips still, your arms will want to flop around as you do the motion. Try your best to keep your hands still, as otherwise they flop around and give the impression you're having a seizure (at least that's what I looked like). Try keeping them pinned to your side.

Make your slides gracely - don't yank your feet back. That has to do with finding the right balance. Put enough weight on your planted foot so that your pivot foot is barely touching the ground. Youre not bringing your feet around in a semi circle fashion in the same sense as with the criss-crosses and alt toe points - instead, you are putting one foot in front of the other in the same fashion as the running man. Keep the half counts tight in the sense of having your up foot pinned next to your down foot. Imagine a two perpendicular planes that each foot goes along back and forth but not off.

Enter into ShimmyWalk by jumping into toe heel stance, one foot in front of the other with rear foot on toe and front foot on heel. Bounce the front foot backward, landing flat - move the rear foot forward tucked next to the other leg, lifting it upwards to about midway between your ankle and your knee. Bring down the up foot and land on your heel, whilst forcing the down foot backwards and raising it slightly so you are on your toes (of the now rear foot). Repeat.

TIPS

Your knees are important - you only get one pair! Be careful not to fall too suddenly into a full squat from standing up. This move is dope, but don't overuse it because of it's potential to put strain on your body.

When you drop down, let yourself free fall for a brief period between when you drop and right before you're about to fall into the squat. As you're free falling, you'll tense up your muscles to soften the drop, which'll give an appearance of your drop being "springy."

Toe stands are hard - don't try to build muscle memory for them by practicing drop downs. Instead, perfect your toe stands seperately, but trying to balance on your toes for extended periods of time, using a wall for balance if necessary. Over time, your toes/feet will get stronger and you'll be able to apply toe stands to any shuffling move you want.

As you rotate your head during the spin, do your best to keep it constrained to two fixed positions, and snap from one to the other. As in, as you spin counter clockwise, look to your left for as long as you can - then, when you've reached the maximal your neck will turn, snap your head around and keep your head affixed facing (what as originally your) right. This also looks more crisp then letting your head fly about.

The sliding motion is the most difficult part to implement in the moonspin. Start by just stepping instead of sliding where applicable, and slowly but surely introduce more and more slide until you are comfortable dragging your foot across your body.

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Step your right foot over to the left of your left foot. As your right foot lands, begin to pivot both feet counter-clockwise. Once you are facing towards what was originally your right, (continue pivoting and)drag your left foot backward across the ground towards your right foot until your feet are shoulder width apart again and facing forward.

Take note of how your kick leg is making a sideways figure 8 (when viewed from your side), with your plant foot intersecting the middle.

As your right leg kicks in a figure 8 motion, your left leg will want to slide back and forth to balance out your stance. Your legs will always be moving in the inverse direction of the other; i.e., as your right (kick) foot is moving outward in either direction, your left (plant) foot will move outwards in the opposite direction. As your kick leg moves inwards from either front or rear, your plant leg will slide inwards from the opposite. Getting these slides of your plant foot takes practice and lots of patience.

Nearly same as rear loopdeloop, but start figure-8 by kicking forward. Read walkthrough on rear loopdeloop to learn the figure 8 motion. After entering the loopdeloop, you can continue the figure 8 kicking motion as many times as you want.
