Your knees are important - you only get one pair! Be careful not to fall too suddenly into a full squat from standing up. This move is dope, but don't overuse it because of it's potential to put strain on your body.

When you drop down, let yourself free fall for a brief period between when you drop and right before you're about to fall into the squat. As you're free falling, you'll tense up your muscles to soften the drop, which'll give an appearance of your drop being "springy."

Toe stands are hard - don't try to build muscle memory for them by practicing drop downs. Instead, perfect your toe stands seperately, but trying to balance on your toes for extended periods of time, using a wall for balance if necessary. Over time, your toes/feet will get stronger and you'll be able to apply toe stands to any shuffling move you want.

Take note of how your kick leg is making a sideways figure 8 (when viewed from your side), with your plant foot intersecting the middle.

As your right leg kicks in a figure 8 motion, your left leg will want to slide back and forth to balance out your stance. Your legs will always be moving in the inverse direction of the other; i.e., as your right (kick) foot is moving outward in either direction, your left (plant) foot will move outwards in the opposite direction. As your kick leg moves inwards from either front or rear, your plant leg will slide inwards from the opposite. Getting these slides of your plant foot takes practice and lots of patience.

Nearly same as rear loopdeloop, but start figure-8 by kicking forward. Read walkthrough on rear loopdeloop to learn the figure 8 motion. After entering the loopdeloop, you can continue the figure 8 kicking motion as many times as you want.

Find the right amount of hop forward with each move - too much and it'll look funky, too little and you're practically just doing the reverse running man. Other than that this move is pretty simple and not too unintuitive.

As if you were doing a normal reverse running man, each time to kick your rear foot forward, keep your front foot up and pinned next to your shin (instead of landing flat on it). With each step backwards, twist your hips as you step behind you to get the full rotation of the 180 motion. This helps you maintain balance and look fluid. With each twist, your body should practically be completely perpendicular to the axis you are moving along.

Per the name of the move, with each hip twist, turn your head over your shoulder (to the side of the foot going back). This will help you maintain balance.

  • 1 Enter from reverse cuts. From mid beat stance (left foot planted, right foot next to shin), step backwards with your right foot, directly behind the left.
  • 2 You should now be in an exaggerated toe heel stance. Drag your right foot forward ever so slightly forward while bringing your left foot up and next to your shin.
  • 3 Step backwards in the same motion as the first step. Keep repeating this motion.

As you are moving forward when you spin, your momentum is pulling you forward and will throw you off balance. As you spin, lean slightly against the direction of your movement to stay upright as you spin instead of keeling over. Leaning against the motion will also allow you to come to a graceful stop instead of falling out of the spin sloppily.

As noted above, your momentum is going to try to pull you out of the spin and throw off your balance. Think of the spin as going directly up vertically and around - instead horizontally in the direction you were moving.

As your left shoulder dips, let your right arm trail behind, horizontal to the ground. As you spin, your right arm should come in a little closer to your body in a hook shape (think oh ballerina's look when they spin), with your hand leading the rest of the arm. Your left arm should be slightly up from next to your side, leading the spin, pulling your momentum forwards.

  • 1 Face forward, feet shoulder width apart.
  • 2 Step to your left with your left foot, dipping your left shoulder down slightly.
  • 3 Bounce forward from your step on your left foot, bringing your right foot around and across your body in a counter-clockwise motion.
  • 4 As your right foot comes across your body, it should dip down with your shoulders then dip back up and come around your body counterclockwise as your rotate your hips into the turn.
  • 5 As you complete the 360, you should slowly come to a stop (you shouldn't be falling into the stop), facing the direction in which you were moving.

Your foot that is trails can either drag across the ground by the toe, or trail in the air around your spin/plant foot. Whichever allows you better balance. As you enter the spin, it's advised you drag your trail foot along the ground by the toe for minimal drag, and to lead into the spin (for aesthetic purposes).

No need to do anything special with your arms with this spin - just let them trail along naturally with the motion of the spin - just don't be too stiff.

  • 1 Start with feet shoulder width apart, facing forward.
  • 2 Take a step to your left sideways with your left foot. As your foot lands: leading with your head, twist your body clockwise with your right leg leaving the ground as you spin.
  • 3 Maintaining the forward momentum from the step forward, continue twisting in your clockwise motion, letting your right foot trail along in the air with the motion of the spin.
  • 4 Once your right leg is all the way to the far left of your motion, you should have brought it down and the spin to a stop.
  • 5 You can now, keeping the momentum going to the left, repeat the above steps, this time spinning counter-clockwise.
